### Hardware Block Diagram

Block diagrams are an excellent way to provide an overarching understanding of a system and the relationships among its individual components. Generally, block diagrams draw from visual modeling languages like the Universal Modeling Language (UML). Each block represents a subsystem, and each connection indicates a relationship between the connected blocks. Typically, the relationship in a system diagram denotes an input-output interaction.

In the block diagram, each subsystem should be depicted by a single block. For each block, there should be a brief explanation of its functional expectations and associated constraints. Similarly, each connection should have a concise description of the relationship it represents, including the nature of the connection (such as power, analog signal, serial communication, or wireless communication) and any relevant constraints.

The end result should present a comprehensive view of a well-defined system, delegating all atomic responsibilities necessary to accomplish the project scope to their respective subsystems.


### Operational Flow Chart

Similar to a block diagram, the flow chart aims to specify the system, but from the user's point of view rather than illustrating the arrangement of each subsystem. It outlines the steps a user needs to perform to use the device and the screens/interfaces they will encounter. A diagram should be drawn to represent this process. Each step should be represented in the diagram to visually depict the sequence of actions and corresponding screens/interfaces the user will encounter while using the device.

## Ethical, Professional, and Standards Considerations

The development of a solar converting retrofit kit presents an affordable, yet efficient and qualitative way for consumers to convert already existing electrically 
powered golf cars into golf cars that run completely on solar renewable energy. This section will evaluate the project's ethical and professional implications, as well as
the responsibilities and standards that must be adhered. This section will also detail the project's broader implications on society, the environment, public health and safety,
as well as the economy. These considerations have influenced the conceptual design of this project, as the design takes into account the constraints and regulations
necessary to ensure a safe retrofit kit that is of excellent quality.

### Ethical Issues to be Considered

There are several glaring ethical issues that may arise during the project's lifespan. The first is ensuring that the customer completely understands both the capabilities
and shortcomings of the retrofit kit. All constraints that the retrofit kit possesses must be properly communicated. Data that has been collected during the course of this
project, including battery life, braking and steering capabilities, and propulsion power must be accurately and effectively communicated to the customer so that there is no
confusion in the performance of the finished product. 

The safety of those that use the retrofit kit in all circumstances must also be remembered. All modifications to the existing golf car, using the retrofit kit, must comply 
with ANSI/OPEI safety and performance requirements for golf cars [1]. Additionally, it is imperative that the installation process of the retrofit kit is also deemed safe, in 
order to ensure those that are working to implement these retrofit kits are kept safe.

### Broader Impacts on Society

The impacts that a retrofit kit would have on society as a whole are mostly positive, bringing an affordable and efficient method to convert an existing battery powered
club car to solar power, with some negatives. 

Cultural and Societal Impacts: The use of golf cars in the modern era can be seen everywhere. College campuses, resorts, retirement communities, and industrial sites all use golf 
cars in one way or another. A retrofit kit to convert these existing golf cars into being powered by solar energy would usher in a new way for electrical vehicle
technology to be used. This would allow the world to be more exposed, in a positive way, to both solar and electric vehicle technologies. Intellectual innovations regarding
both solar and electric vehicle technologies would follow, allowing for improved designs and products in these areas of study. 

Environmental Impacts: A solar powered conversion kit would have both positive and negative impacts on the environment. Positives include reduced reliance on grid charging stations
for battery powered electric vehicles. Battery life would also be extended for such vehicles, assuming ideal weather conditions. Carbon emissions on a local scale would be reduced, as there would be less gas powered vehicles in use, as well. Negative environmental impacts would include the emissions created from the increased production of the solar panels in use by such retrofit kits. Extracting materials necessary for the production of these conversion kits would also create increased emissions on the environment. End of life disposal of batteries being used by these solar powered vehicles is a concern. It is paramount that used batteries be disposed of in a proper and professional manner. 

Economic Impacts: Converting an already existing battery powered golf car to being solar powered would be cheaper than the alternative of purchasing an entirely new golf car. For companies looking to mass-convert existing golf cars, it would also be an efficient and cost-effective way to convert large amounts of golf cars in a short period of time. 
Such a retrofit kit would also create a large amount of jobs, as there would be many more tasks that would need to be fulfilled to achieve the successful implementation of these kits. These jobs would appear all over the economy. Jobs would be created to gather the resources necessary for the production of these retrofit kits, as well as jobs in manufacturing, in order to create more things that the kit would possess, such as solar panels, solar-rechargeable batteries, arms to hold said solar panel, and any extra wiring needed for the implementation of the new battery. There would also be an increase in skilled labor, as jobs must be created to both install and maintain these retrofit kits.

Public Safety Impacts: Safety is of the utmost importance in a project such as this, that requires potentially fatal amounts of electricity to operate. All constraints and
specifications listed in the ***Fully Formulated Problem*** section must be followed to ensure the safety of all involved in the design, implementation, and consumption process of this retrofit kit.

Public Health Impacts: Carbon emissions would be reduced on a local scale by a considerable amount for consumers looking to convert fleets of golf cars into being solar powered. These reduced emissions would have a positive effect on the community, creating cleaner air for inhabitants. On a cognitive level, the development of a retrofit kit could potentially inspire increased research and innovations on technologies involved in the retrofit kit system.

## Resources

You have already estimated the resources needed to complete the solution. Now, let's refine those estimates.

### Budget

Develop a budget proposal with justifications for expenses associated with each subsystem. Note that the total of this budget proposal can also serve as a specification for each subsystem. After creating the budgets for individual subsystems, merge them to create a comprehensive budget for the entire solution.

### Division of Labor

First, conduct a thorough analysis of the skills currently available within the team, and then compare these skills to the specific requirements of each subsystem. Based on this analysis, appoint a team member to take the specifications for each subsystem and generate a corresponding solution (i.e. detailed design). If there are more team members than subsystems, consider further subdividing the solutions into smaller tasks or components, thereby allowing each team member the opportunity to design a subsystem.

### Timeline

Revise the detailed timeline (Gantt chart) you created in the project proposal. Ensure that the timeline is optimized for detailed design. Address critical unknowns early and determine if a prototype needs to be constructed before the final build to validate a subsystem. Additionally, if subsystem $A$ imposes constraints on subsystem $B$, generally, subsystem $A$ should be designed first.
